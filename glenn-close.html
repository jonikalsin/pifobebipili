Table of Biography

Glenn Close is an American film and stage actress. Glenn Close is best known for her roles in ‘Fatal Attraction’ and ‘Dangerous Liaisons.

Early Life

Glenn Close was born to William Close (father) and Bettine Close(mother) on March 19, 1947. She is 76 years old right now. Her family was an elite upper-class family in Connecticut.

She has three siblings. Her parent’s Moral Re-Armament(MRA). It was a cult group that sent its members all over the world to convert people. Hence, Glenn and her family moved to Congo when she was 13.

Glenn attended Rosemary Hall Preparatory School in Greenwich. Moreover, she also joined ‘Up With People’. It is an MRA singing group with whom she traveled all over Europe and the US.

Later, she left MRA and joined the College of William and Mary. She studied drama and theatre. Moreover, she also developed her acting skills in the college’s theatre department.

Glenn Close – Net Worth 2024

Glenn has worked has enough in her career to earn a good reputation and wealth as well. Her net worth is around $50 million.

Awards

In 1982, Glenn won The National Board of Review Award for Best Supporting Actress for her debut movie ‘The World According to Garp’. She received the German Golden Camera Award for Best International Actress for ‘Dangerous Liaisons.

Moreover, in 2004, she was presented with The Golden Globe Award for Best Actress Miniseries or Television Film for ‘The Lion in Winter’. Glenn also won The Golden Globe Award for Best Actress-Television Series Drama for ‘Damages’ in 2007.

Moreover, she won the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress- Drama Series for the same role in 2007.

Personal Life

Glenn Close has been married three times. However, all her marriages ended in divorce. She married Cabot Wade (1969-1971), Janes Marlas (1984-1987), and David Shaw (2006-2015).

Moreover, she has a romantic relationship with John Starke. They also have a daughter together. In addition, she founded BringChange2Mind, a campaign to end the stigma and discrimination faced by people suffering from mental illness.

Professional Life

Glenn Close joined the Phoenix Theatre Company after moving to New York. In 1974, she began her career with a Broadway play, ‘Love for Love’. At the start of her career, she loved doing theatre. She did not think about going to the movies. However, later she eventually did.

Hollywood Debut

In 1982, Glenn made her Hollywood debut with the comedy-drama ‘The World According to Garp’. She was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her role. Moreover, she also acted in ‘The Big Chill’ in 1983, ‘The Natural’ in 1984, and ‘Maxie’ in 1985.

In 1987, she acted in ‘Fatal Attraction’ as Alex Forrest. It was her first highly unconventional role. In 1988, ‘Dangerous Liaisons’ established her talent for playing on-screen vamps.

Movies such as ‘Hamlet'(1990), ‘Meeting Venus'(1991), ‘The Paper'(1994), and ‘Mars Attacks!'(1996) proved very productive for Glenn. However, her most memorable role was in the movie ‘101 Dalmatians'(1996) as Cruella De Vil.

Glenn also worked in television. She appeared in shows such as ‘Sarah, Plain and Tall(1991), ‘Skylark'(1993), and ‘In the Gloaming'(1997).

She played the role of Cruella De Vil in the movie ‘102 Dalmatians’. It was her new start of the millennium. Her other movies of the decade include ‘South Pacific'(2001), ‘Le Divorce'(2003), ‘Heights'(2004), ‘Nine Lives'(2005), and ‘Evening'(2007).

She was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Actress. Moreover, she won the Golden Globe Award for her performance in the movie.
